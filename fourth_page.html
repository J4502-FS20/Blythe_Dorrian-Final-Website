Resources for help

Mental health is a significant problem within the pandemic. Being inside with only a few people, or being alone can be hard on people. Sometimes people have turned to unhealthy habits. People have also been facing changing eating and sleeping habits, and it makes coping harder.

According to the World Health Organization, approximately 100 million people in the US suffer from a mental health issue. Increasing anxiety of the health of others is a rising issue in Americans.

Data has reported that mental health needs are increasing. During the week of July 16-21, 44.6% of women, and 37% of men reported symptoms of depression or anxiety.

National contacts: Phone numbers

The substance abuse help number is 1-800-642-HELP. This is available for people suffering from any kind of addictions. Breaking habits can be tough, and these people are not alone.

The 24 Hour Crisis Hotline is (844)-942-2459.

The Mental Health Hotline's phone number is 1-800-662-4357 (HELP)

Report domestic violence at 1-800-799-7233 if you have a partner you are living with and they are acting offensive toward you.

In-person counseling places in Columbia, MO

Aspire Counseling Center is located on 302 Campusview Drive in Columbia. Their phone number is (573) 328-2288.

If you need relationship counseling, the Counseling Hub's phone number is 573) 586-3204. They are located on 601 W. Nifong Blvd. suite #1B.

The Mizzou Counseling Center's phone number is (573)-882-6601. They are an open resource for students and faculty, and they are close to downtown. They are located on 406 S. 6th St.

The Children's Therapy Center can be reached at (573) 882-7350. They specialize in adolescents and the mental health of children. Their address is 525 N. Keene St. The hospital is near the Women and Children's Hospital, which is about eight minutes from Mizzou.
