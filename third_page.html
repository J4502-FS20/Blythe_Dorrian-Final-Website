Trends around the US

Exercise levels have been a studied topic for years even before the pandemic began earlier this year. This worldwide spread has been continued since everything has been closing.

The WHO noted that smartphone apps that track steps and heart rate have dropped 50% since March 2020. A study published on June 29 said that 19,144,639 measurements have still been taken from 187 countries.

According to the CDC, 23% of Americans get the required amount of exercise per week. Children spend more than seven hours in front of a screen.

In the Midwest, the trends are slightly higher. Missouri has a 23.6% average, Kansas has a 23.2%. This is still slightly above average.

The states based on their percentage of people who get the required amount of exercise.
