Trends around the US

Exercise levels have been a studied topic for years even before the pandemic began earlier this year. This worldwide spread has been continued since everything has been closing.

Studies have been tracked around the US and the world. The University of California- San Fransisco noted that smartphone apps that track steps and heart rate have dropped 50% since March 2020. A study published on June 29 said that 19,144,639 measurements have still been taken from 187 countries.

Diminishing levels have been observed as well. the Department of Health and Human Services said that less than 5% of Americans get the required amount of 30 minutes of exercise per day. More than 80% of adults don't get the required muscle strengthening activity.

It was noted from the same study that back in March, outdoor levels of exercise started to slowly increase. New data is being tracked to see how changes are happening.

The map above from the National Health Interview Survey in 2010-2015 describes the state's average of exercise. The national average is 22.9%.

In the Midwest, the trends are slightly higher. Missouri has a 23.6% average, Kansas has a 23.2%. This is still slightly above average.

The states in red are significantly lower than average. Light pink means they are below average, but it is not a significant amount. Light blue means they are greater than average, but not significantly. Lastly, dark blue means they are significantly above average.

The south has some of the lower rates of exercise compared to the rest of the country, while the west coast typically has higher rates. However, there is a mix of rates throughout the country.
